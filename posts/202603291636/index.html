Body Route

Routing the electric guitar body

2026-3-29 21:36

Exciting times.

The guitar body can finally take shape.

First the outline is roughed out on the bandsaw. I left about 1/8 inch all around. If I had to do this again I would try to get closer, as even this is a lot of extra wood to remove by router.

Routing

I am using a 1/2 inch diameter by 1 inch deep top-bearing bit. This will require two passes to get the full 1.5 inch depth. This produced a ridiculous amount of dust and shavings.

Some shots of the first go round:

Now for the final pass.

Only two screw ups

Around the upper horn, the router jumped a little and caused a couple minor gouges in the side. I think/hope these will sand out.

The other boo boo is visible in the far right of this photo of the end. I do not think it was a routing error, but something went wrong with the glue up of the body sections and has caused a chunk to have broken out. I will have to fill this with an off-cut of the cherry section wood and try to smooth it out, but it will always be visible to some degree.

All other imperfections are no worry for now and will likely sand down nicely.

The Result

This is looking awesome if I do say so myself.

I would not do this again without using a table-mounted router.
