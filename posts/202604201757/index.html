Finish-sanding and applying the first seal coat of shellac

2026-4-20 22:58

Now the entire guitar is sanded end to end.

First, 150 grit is used to remove any remaining carving marks and to fix any remaining contour and shaping issues.

This is followed by a thorough going-over with 220 grit.

All scratches must be eliminated.

Then another round of 360.

Finally a light pass with 600 and 1000 grits. Possibly overkill, but I like the result.

Now one seal-coat of 1-pound-cut platina shellac is applied.

Looking pretty decent.

This seal coat will be kept on for the remainder of the build, the fret installation, and the test-playing period when the electronics circuit is finalized. It will be again sanded to 1000 grit before the final French Polish shellac finish is applied.
