Body Assembly

Assembling the body of the neck-through guitar

2026-3-23 13:40

First to plug the temporary air holes leading into the hollow chambers.

Some 3/8 plugs of flat sawn walnut are glued in.

Chiseled flush.

Jointing the edges

Now for the task of getting the joints to mate perfectly. This is done almost entirely with the #7 plane. I use a .0025 feeler gauge to check the seam for any gaps.

At one point I tried to fix some high and low spots with a card scraper. This never worked perfectly. Best to trust the plane. When the plane finally takes off a long shaving of the full length of the joint, it is right. Only one or two places and the ends where the thin metal feeler gauge gets through. At some point you have to stop and say it is as good as it will get.

This step was probably the most challenging part of the entire build so far.

Dowels to aid in glueing

I use some short, no longer than 3/8 of an inch, 1/4 inch oak dowels to keep the joints in place while gluing. These are only to keep the pieces from sliding around during the glue up. Not for any structural reinforcement. They are kept very short and the holes as shallow as possible to prevent future stress on the wood.

Both sides just about ready!

Blue side

I glued the blue side first. I was able to use my 8 inch C clamps here, which apply a great deal of pressure and ensure any imperfections in the seam are pressed together flat.

This side joint had some gap issues near the lower end which press together almost completely with the strong C clamps. I stuck some maple plane shavings into the glue of any remaining gap, which should blend right in with the wood invisibly.

The operation was a success!

Red side

Now for the red side. Too wide for any C clamps, so I had to use three bar clamps. Pipe clamps would have been better. Fortunately, this side joint mated very closely and did not required excessive pressure. Although I did tighten the bar clamps as much as possible, to the point the the bars really flexed.

The Result

Overall I am happy with how this turned out. It is starting to look like something.

Some work with the plane again to get the top flat. Curly tiger maple is looking fantastic.

Major milestone reached!

Next up: routing.

Only one more glue joint to make on the entire guitar.
