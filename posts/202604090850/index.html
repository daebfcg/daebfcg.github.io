Fretboard Removal

Un-gluing the misaligned fretboard

2026-4-9 13:50

Here is where we landed with the failed glue-down. It is misaligned by about 1/16 inch at the body end. I have decided that it must be removed and reattached. Good thing I used hide glue.

Also good is that most of the wood shown here is going to be carved or sanded away eventually, so any injuries during the removal process are not a likely major cause of concern.

Step 1 is to protect the body and headstock areas with some heavy masking paper.

Ungluing Tools

  1. Wallpaper steamer
  2. Heat gun
  3. Putty knife (with corners filed round)
  4. Cake frosting spatula

Sometimes just the heat gun and spatula work. For bigger jobs like this that require more time and heat, some steam is good to keep things flexible. The steam from this Wagner power steamer really penetrates and gets the glue moving again.

Process

The first thing to look for is a corner where the putty knife or spatula can get hold.

I start with the heat gun to heat things up overall. (Note use of aluminum foil to protect wood from heat gun.) I ended up just using the steamer and putty knife for the remainder of this job.

Gradually it lifts up.

And it is off! It looks like a big mess, but no harm done.

Clean up

The fretboard got rather bent. While still hot and wet, I cleaned off as much glue as I could and clamped it flat to cool and dry.

The neck cleans cleans up quite well, too. The thin fillet of wood covering the truss rod got destroyed, though, but I can redo that easily enough.
