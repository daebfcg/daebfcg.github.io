Red Side Body Assembly

Assembling one side of the guitar including shielding the control cavity

2026-3-5 22:19

Instead of referring to right and left sides while building a guitar, I have adopted a red/blue terminology to avoid any confusion that can occur when viewing from the back or upside down. Instead of marking L or R, I always just use either a red or blue dot.

This post will be all about putting together the red side.

To prepare for assembly I planed the top and back plates flat. To do this, I screwed them directly to my bench. (I will fill in the holes later.) This photo shows the inside surfaces of the top plates being planed.

The plates are aligned with the inner core using 1/4 inch oak dowels in waste areas. This locks them into place while gluing.

The top plate is glued down. I used a section of 4x4 douglas fir post held in the vise for a clamping jig.

Now that the the top is glued down I can prepare the control cavity. As is, it will be 3/4 inch deep. The top plate is 3/8 thick, which is too thick for the pot and switch posts so will need to be thinned. To thin the top plate I routed out an extra 1/8 inch using a round nose bit to make a nice rounded inside corner. The top plate is now 1/4 inch thick over the control cavity.

Next I sealed the cavity floor and walls with shellac. Then applied copper shielding tape over all the inner surfaces. The shielding tape will form a Faraday cage to prevent noise. It will also be connected to ground, and will automatically ground any components touching it such as metal pot and switch bodies.

Now to cut the access hole in the back plate.

The access hole will be covered by a sheet of 12 gauge brass. The cover plate will be a bit larger all around and will have a shallow recess for it to sit in and provide a surface for it to be screwed down to. To make the recess, I made an acrylic routing template and used a small 1/8 deep flush trim router bit.

As a final touch I rounded over the inside edge of the hole in the back plate, sealed it where it covers the control cavity and applied copped shielding there too. The control cavity is fully shielded!

The back plate was then glued down using the same method as the top plate.
