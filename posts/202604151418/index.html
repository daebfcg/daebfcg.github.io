Fretboard Radius

Sanding the fretboard to a 12 inch radius

2026-4-14 21:18

The guitar is positioned and supported so that the neck remains flat and does not flex during the sanding process. Some protective paper is used to cover the body and headstock.

The neck support block used here is a rolling-pin holder I picked up from a flea market for $3. StewMac sells the same thing, lined with cork, for over $20.

A StewMac 12 inch radius sanding block is used. (A longer radius beam would be ideal.) A section of 80 grit sanding-belt is stuck to the concave surface with double-sided tape.

The important thing is to ensure that the fretboard surface remains flat and of equal thickness all around during sanding.

This is followed up with 220 grit. I will take another few passes with 400-grit before installing the frets.

The fretboard now has a nice 12 inch radius across the top.

Fortunately any overly deep dot inlays have now come to the surface, and the slotting-mishap scratch at the 20th fret has completely disappeared.

The side dots are also centered now, so my calculations were correct in placing them slightly below center.

A knotty spot around the 8th and 9th frets has been revealed, and the wood there seems hard yet more brittle than typical walnut. I think this will be ok, and hopefully made durable with the application of Tung oil. I am a little worried though. Will the guitar need a fretboard replacement someday?
