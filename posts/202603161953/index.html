Fretboard Part 6

Attaching the edge binding to the fretboard

2026-3-17 00:53

Now to prepare the fretboard and binding for the glue up.

To being with, I create a layer consisting of masking tape, double sided tape, and craft paper on the bottom of the fretboard. This is so that the binding strips extend down past the bottom edge when being glued down, creating some excess binding on the bottom. This is to try to avoid the binding not coming all the way down to the bottom edge of the fretboard which would be disastrous.

To keep glue out of the slots I wedge some folded Tyvek into each slot, as well as tape off the nut slot.

Each strip is given a final sanding down to 1000 grit and cleaned of any dust.

First the bottom end binding is glued on, made from an off-cut of one of the strips.

Each side piece is glued on, one at a time. The jig is simply a couple strips of particle board screwed together to form a corner with some cork added to the inside edge.

Note use of binding tape to help pull tight, and auxiliary hold-down clamps to keep fretboard flat.

Now all three edges have binding. What looks rough now will soon clean up.

Here is a shot of the bottom showing the excess binding coming proud of the edge as I intended, thanks to the backer layer.

The excess is planed off the top and bottom using a micro plane, and sanded smooth.

Finally, the edges are sanded smooth and square again, taking off the extra 1/32 of an inch and making the bindings their target 1/16 inch thickness. The fretboard is now at the target dimensions, with the exception of the radius across the top. There is also a bit of extra length at the headstock end which I am leaving on for now until final placement on the neck is determined.

Done with the fretboard until final assembly!
