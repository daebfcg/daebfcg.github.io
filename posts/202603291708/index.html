Fretboard Placement

Locking in the placement of the fretboard

2026-3-29 22:08

Now I will get the fretboard perfectly lined up and in locked into its final position in preparation for it to be glued down. Here is the method I have settled on.

Positioning

This is just a matter of lining up the center line of the fretboard with the guitar neck and body center line, and ensuring the zero-fret is at the predetermined measurement at the headstock end.

The fretboard is clamped firmly in place.

Dowel Indexing

The fretboard will be locked into position on the neck with two short dowels. This is crucial to prevent the fretboard from sliding out of position during gluing. I have used small brads into two frets before, but I have found they are unreliable and prone to movement from clamping forces and cause slippage. I now use 3/16 dowels instead, which are much more stable and keep the position locked in.

To get the dowel holes in the bottom of the fretboard and top face of the neck perfectly matched, I start by drilling 3/64 diameter holes using a hand crank drill. The holes are drilled in the fret slots of course so will not be visible once the frets are installed.

Here I am using the first and twentieth frets. These resulting holes are then drilled to about 3/32 deep using a 3/16 brad point bit. Now some sort sections of dowels can be used to lock the position.

The important thing here is to not drill into the truss rod channel or carbon fiber bar, so I need to come out about 9/16 from the center line.

I am using a hand powered drill and I made a little jig from acrylic to keep the bit straight up and down, being too lazy to go transfer this whole assembly to the drill press.

Now with two short 3/16 dowels in the underside of the fretboard, they lock into their respective holes in the neck. I can take it on and off multiple times and it always goes back into the exact spot, which is handy.

Preview

Here is a preview of things to come. Still a lot of excess wood to be removed from the neck, but I am keeping it there for now so it stays square and easy to hold in the vise.

UPDATE: Use 1/4 or 5/16 oak dowels, preferably more than two. The 3/16 poplar dowel gets too soft when the glue hits it and does not hold well.
