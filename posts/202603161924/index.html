Fretboard Part 5

Making the side dots

2026-3-17 00:25

I am going to try adding the side dots to the player-side (blue side) fretboard edge binding before attaching the binding.

For the dots, I am using the curly maple wood used on the rest of the guitar.

Here is the process I have come up with for making wood side dots.

First I cut out ten small blocks and glue them to the ends of dowels with CA gel glue. The grain direction is important and I want the quartersawn side facing out, not the end grain. (Otherwise I would have just used lengthwise sticks.)

I mark the grain direction up side, and roughly sand the blocks to match the cylindrical shape of the dowel.

These are then sharpened to conical points using a pencil sharpener.

Now the 10 points are finely sanded and ready for installation.

The 3/32 diameter holes for the dots are then drilled into a binding strip at the precise locations of the intended fret marker locations.

The vertical position of the line of dots is slightly below center to take into account the resulting fretboard thickness at the edges after being sanded to a 12 inch radius.

Now the dot points are glued into the holes. I do this in two sessions since the 12th fret double-dot markers are too close to be done at the same time. (This explains why I made five double-ended dowels instead of ten.)

The points are then cut off flush and sanded, making some nice looking dots.

Now this binding strip and the matching strip for the other side are ready to be glued to the fretboard.
