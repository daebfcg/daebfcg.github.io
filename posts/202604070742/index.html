Truss Rod Installation

Setting the truss rod into the neck for final time

2026-4-7 12:42

Before installing the truss rod I would like to cover up the bottom of this drill hole to make it look nicer when someone opens the cover to the adjustment access port.

A round piece of maple veneer works nicely. I like to add a little note to whoever will be doing the adjustment and let them know for sure what size allen wrench to use.

Preview of how it will appear with the fretboard installed and the adjustment cover removed. (The truss rod adjustment cover plate will be fabricated later.)

Setting the truss rod

The truss rod fits in snugly, but I am opting to put a few dabs of silicone caulk under it to prevent any rattles. One dab on each end and one in the middle. It is imperative to not get any silicone on the gluing surface, hence the masking tape. I have already sealed the truss rod channel with shellac so am not worried about the silicone seeping through into the neck wood. Silicone must never get in or on bare wood. I hardly ever touch the stuff.

Once installed, it is clamped down tightly and left overnight to let the silicone fully cure.

The truss rod is in for good.

It is a little deep, and has left a very slight gap between where it will hit against the bottom of the fretboard. Not show, but I glued in a thin fillet of spruce to fill the gap.
