Blue Side Body Assembly

Assembling the remaining half of the body

2026-3-13 22:47

Now to assemble the blue side.

The chambers are first cut out of the center section. I am making the total volume of these chambers bit less than the red side chamber. The red side control cavity ended up being large and taking out a lot of wood. I want to leave a bit more solid mass around the bridge.

First the back plate is glued down.

After gluing the back plate, I noticed much glue had oozed into the chambers. I was able to cleanly remove it all. But after attaching the top any excess glue that squeezes into the chambers will be impossible to remove. Plus, being mostly air tight the blobs of glue might never cure and end up molding inside there. So I need to come up with a strategy to prevent this.

My plan is three-fold:

  1. Mask off the inside of the top plate where it covers the chamber so that no glue gets applied there, and remove the masking tape just before attaching.

  2. Drill a 3/8 diameter hole from the inside edge of the body into each chamber. This will allow air to get into the chamber and ensure that any glue that does get trapped inside cures properly. These holes will be plugged back up with walnut wood plugs after a week or so of drying time.

  3. Fill the chambers with strips of Tyvek paper that can be pulled out through the aforementioned air holes. These should catch any excess glue that might get squeezed into the chambers.

The air holes drilled:

Filled with Tyvek strips:

The great thing about Tyvek is that it is nearly impossible to tear, so when I pull the strips out I do not have to worry about pieces ripping off and getting stuck inside.

The final glue up:

I pulled out the Tyvek strips after about an hour. Hardly any glue came out with them, so I am hoping the squeeze out into the chambers was minimal.

The assembled side.
