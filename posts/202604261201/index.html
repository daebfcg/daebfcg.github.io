Truss rod cover, control plate cover, and string ferrules

2026-4-26 17:01

Truss Rod Cover

A small cover plate goes over the truss-rod adjustment port.

This is made from a headstock offcut (so that it blends in visually with the neck laminations) with a slice of cherry glued to the back of it laid horizontally for strength. Overall it is about 3/32 thick.

The two screws land at the 24 fret position.

String Ferrules

Black string-through ferrules from Philadelphia Luthier Tools, made of solid brass.

The holes in the back of the guitar need to be drilled out to 3/8 wide by 3/8 deep. The drill press pin-from-the-other-side technique is not working for me since the 3/8 brad point bit is so long it wanders due to runout on the drill press. To get the holes centered correctly I had to plug the existing 1/8 holes and reestablish the center points.

Then carefully make them 3/8 deep so the ferrules are flush with the back.

They are a tight fit and need to be tapped in with a hammer and wooden dowel.

I will remove them before finishing, and reinstall again afterwards, possibly gluing in with a drop of CA in each.

Only a slight amount of tear-out on the third string hole in the walnut which had to be sanded out. Otherwise, not too bad.

Control Cavity Cover

I was originally planning to make this out of a sheet of 12 gauge brass. Looking around for something to use as a material for a temporary cover, I found something that works and is good enough for the real thing.

Someone might get a smile someday when they open this up to work on it.

Polished up it looks pretty good.

Back fittings are done!
