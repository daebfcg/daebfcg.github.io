Fretboard Part 3

Tapering the fretboard

2026-3-16 21:49

The fretboard can now be tapered to 1/8 inch narrower than the final width. This is to account for the 1/16 inch edge binding that will be attached to each side.

The taper lines are based on the target overall string-to-string width at the zero fret: 1.44 inches, and the target string width at the bridge: 2.07 inches. From these string lines, I move out 1/16 inch on each side so that once the 1/16 thick bindings are attached there will be 1/8 overhang of fretboard from the outer strings. The width of the fretboard at the zero-fret will end up being about 1.6875 (or 1 and 11/16) which is pretty standard.

Once the cut lines are determined, I tape off the area to keep.

The waste edges are cut off nearly to the tape line on the bandsaw, and then I sneak up to the lines on my disc sander.

Finally, the edges are sanded flat and straight on the soapstone slab (an old antique cabinet top) keeping the edge square.

Ready for binding.
