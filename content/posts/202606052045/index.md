My finish of choice for luthiery is shellac.  It is completely natural and non toxic and enhances the chatoyance of wood like no other finish.  It is extremely thin so lets the wood vibrate and does not dampen the resonance of the instrument (unless excessive coats are applied.)

It comes in flake form.

{{<imgresize shellac_flake.jpg "800x800" "Flakes">}}

I make small, 1 ounce batches.  To make a "one pound cut" mixture I use 1/8 ounce of shellac and 1 ounce of spirit (alcohol).

{{<imgresize weighing_flakes.jpg "800x800" "Weighing flakes">}}

The best spirit to use is Everclear 190 proof grain alcohol.

{{<imgresize spirit.jpg "800x800" "Spirit">}}

I do not drink it but I heard you can.

These are combined in a jar and left tightly sealed up overnight for the flake to dissolve.  

{{<imgresize jar.jpg "800x800" "Shellac jar">}}

The freshly mixed up shellac can be used the next day.  It lasts a month or two, but since I use small batches I usually use it up.  Any leftover goes into another jar that can be used for sealing timbers.

An entire instrument takes about two batches (2 oz total).
