First a new spruce filler cap over the truss rod is glued in, and then planed down flush.

{{<imgresize filler_cap.jpg "800x800" "Spruce filler cap over truss rod">}}

Fretboard and neck ready to glue again, with new alignment dowels.

{{<imgresize ready.jpg "800x800" "Neck ready for fretboard">}}

This time around I used a traditional fretboard clamping caul, which is made from plywood and particleboard with a cork backing.

The advantage of this method is that the clamps are placed carefully and tightened in an even and controlled way.  

{{<imgresize clamping_1.jpg "800x800" "Clamping the fretboard 1">}}

{{<imgresize clamping_2.jpg "800x800" "Clamping the fretboard 2">}}

I suspect that my previous failed attempts were due to uneven clamping pressure exerting a pulling force to one side which caused the slippage.

A possible drawback of using this type of caul is that it may bend slightly under clamping pressure and not end up perfectly flat.  Next time I might use a small slab of stone or something like that as the caul, which would be be ideal.  I think this may not necessarily be crucial since I will be sanding the fretboard to a radius shape and leveling it in the process.

## Results

From this angle, the fretboard center-line is dead on with the body center.

{{<imgresize result.jpg "800x800" "Fretboard alignment after gluing">}}

Looking down the body and neck to check for alignment, things look pretty good.

{{<imgresize view_2.jpg "800x800" "Alignment view">}}

{{<imgresize view_1.jpg "800x800" "Alignment view">}}

Overall a satisfactory result, although maybe not 100% perfect. This is the first time I have glued a fretboard to a guitar with the neck and body already attached and it does required more alignment precision than when gluing to a separate neck.

**The last glue joint to be made on this guitar is now done.**

## Remaining Steps

(Approximate order of operations)

1. Carve the neck to shape
1. Radius-sand the fretboard and clean up fretboard-to-headstock transition
1. Install frets
1. Install bridges and string-through ferrules
1. Make the truss rod cover plate
1. Make the control cavity cover plate
1. Sand down to finish-grit and apply first wash-coat of shellac
1. Treat fretboard with tung-oil
1. Level and crown frets
1. Install tuners and nut
1. Do basic setup
1. Install pickups
1. Experiment with electronics circuit designs
1. Drill holes for pots and switches
1. Solder together electronics components and test
1. Remove all removable parts and apply final French-Polish finish
1. Put all back together and do final setup
