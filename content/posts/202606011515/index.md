First successful attempt at making some wood knobs.

The key to knobs that fit and stay secure on pots are these brass set-screw inserts.

{{<imgresize inserts.jpg "800x800" "Set-screw knob inserts">}}

I started by gluing up four, one-inch blocks of maple.  Did not get a picture of that, but after it was turned on the lathe it looked like this:

{{<imgresize lathed.jpg "800x800" "Blocks turned on a lathe">}}

These were sawn apart into their respective blanks and disc-sanded to a consistent height.  These are 5/8 inch tall.

{{<imgresize blanks.jpg "800x800" "Knob blanks">}}

Why not just make them from a long dowel?  Because I want the tops to be side grain, not end grain.

Next the pilot holes are drilled in the bottom center, and the holes in the sides for the set screws.  The set screw holes are 7/64 inch (and are 3/16 up from the bottom).

{{<imgresize pilot_holes.jpg "800x800" "Pilot holes">}}

{{<imgresize set_screw_holes.jpg "800x800" "Holes for the set screws">}}

The skirting recess is drilled out of the bottom first, using a 3/4 inch forstener bit.  This goes in about 1/8 deep.  The purpose of this is so that the knobs can sit flush on the guitar face, but cover over the washer and nut of the pots.

{{<imgresize skirt_holes.jpg "800x800" "Recess for skirting">}}

Not shown, but finally the hole for the insert is drilled.  This is a 3/8 wide hole.

Once the inserts are tapped in the knob can be installed on a dowel and put in the drill press for sanding.

{{<imgresize sanding.jpg "800x800" "Sanding knob on drill press">}}

{{<imgresize sanded.jpg "800x800" "Knobs sanded smooth">}}

The finish is ONE COAT of Tried-and-True Danish oil.

{{<imgresize finished.jpg "800x800" "Finished knobs">}}

{{<imgresize finished_bottom.jpg "800x800" "Bottom view">}}

They look decent and work well on the Super K.  Much better than the cheap plastic speed knobs.

{{<imgresize on_guitar.jpg "600x600" "Knobs on guitar">}}

{{<imgresize on_guitar_close.jpg "600x600" "Knobs on guitar - detail">}}

These knobs are rather basic.  I would like to try adding numbers somehow, or maybe a "pointer" of contrasting wood, and maybe shaping them a little more than just simple cylinder.  Super K II will get the next iteration of knobs when the times comes.
