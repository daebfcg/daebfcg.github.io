The pickup slots can now be routed to their final depth.

{{<imgresize routing.jpg "800x800" "Routing">}}

I am making the slots 9/16 inch deep to give plenty of room for the wires and mounting hardware below each pickup.

## Pickup wire channels

Holes leading into the control cavity will also need to be made now.  My plan is to use 3-wire JST SM connectors from the pickup lead wires to the circuit test harness, to make disconnection and reconnection easy during the circuit testing process.  I was thinking of keeping the connectors also to be able to remove the pickups and/or control component assembly separately without having to de-solder the connections at later dates.  

{{<imgresize jst_sm_3_connectors.jpg "800x800" "3-wire JST SM connector male/female pair">}}

I am not sure if I will keep this permanently though, as these connectors might introduce a point of noise or failure in the future, and opt to directly solder all connections once the circuit design is finalized.

The male JST SM connectors can just fit through a 3/8 inch hole, so that is the size of wiring channel I am drilling out of the sides of the pickup slots.
