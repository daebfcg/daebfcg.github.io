Now the entire guitar is sanded end to end.

{{<imgresize sanding_1.jpg "800x800" "Sanding">}}

First, 150 grit is used to remove any remaining carving marks and to fix any remaining contour and shaping issues.

{{<imgresize sanding_2.jpg "800x800" "Sanding">}}

This is followed by a thorough going-over with 220 grit.

All scratches must be eliminated.

{{<imgresize sanding_3.jpg "800x800" "Sanding">}}

Then another round of 360.  

{{<imgresize sanding_4.jpg "800x800" "Sanding">}}

 Finally a light pass with 600 and 1000 grits.  Possibly overkill, but I like the result.

{{<imgresize sanding_5.jpg "800x800" "Sanding">}}

Now one seal-coat of 1-pound-cut platina shellac is applied.

{{<imgresize shellacing.jpg "800x800" "Applying shellac">}}

Looking pretty decent.

{{<imgresize full_length_view.jpg "600x600" "Full view of guitar with first coat of shellac">}}

{{<imgresize detail.jpg "800x800" "detail of guitar body with shellac">}}

{{<imgresize end.jpg "800x800" "detail of guitar  end with shellac">}}

This seal coat will be kept on for the remained of the build, the fret installation, and the test-playing period when the electronics circuit is finalized.  It will be again sanded to 1000 grit before the final French Polish shellac finish is applied.
