The pickups are here!

{{<imgresize pickups.jpg "800x800" "The pickups">}}

As planned, these are the quarter pound strat pickups, with optional tap wires.  One is a reverse-wind, reverse-polarity (RWRP) so they will be hum-cancelling when used together in series.

The white Seymour Duncan logos are going to be removed.  Sorry Seymour.

Now the slots can be routed into the guitar body.  This is another example of why it is important to wait until the parts are on hand.  These are measuring slightly larger than their online specs.

## In search of the perfect 3/4 inch wide hotdog

The goal now is to create a routing template of the ideal proportion.  I figure a slot of .75 high by 3.3875 wide will give about 1/32 gap around the edge.  This will be made from the 1/4 inch thick acrylic.  This took me three tries to get close enough to what I consider acceptable.  I started by boring the two end holes with a 3/4 inch forstener bit.  Then the challenge is to connect the holes precisely.  On the third try, I used the router indexed to the edge of the acrylic sheet to cut the slot edges.

## Placement

The locations of the pickup slots are plotted onto the guitar, taking care to ensure they are centered and at the proper angle.   The angle is purely for visual consistency with the two string scale lengths.  The bridge pickup is 14 degrees, and the neck is 11.21 degrees.  These angles are based on the how the frets would lay if extended down this far.

## Routing

At this stage, I am not going to route the slots to their final depth.  The goal now is to route them just deep enough so that the final depth can be routed later without the template, and the router bit bearing can roll on the existing sides.   The reason for this is that once the guitar is cut out there will be no good place to attach the template.  But I also want to screw down the template into the waste wood of the pickup slots.  So I need to preserve some waste wood in the these slot areas for now.  Had to do some thinking to plan this out.
