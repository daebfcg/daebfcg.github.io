The guitar is positioned and supported so that the neck remains straight and does not flex during the sanding process.  Some protective paper is used to cover the body and headstock.

{{<imgresize protection.jpg "800x800" "Protection and support for radius sanding">}}

The neck support block is a rolling-pin holder from a flea market for $3.  StewMac sells the same thing, lined with cork, for over $20.

A StewMac 12 inch radius sanding block is used.  A longer radius beam would be ideal.  Some 80 grit paper is stuck to the concave surface.  The important thing is to ensure that the fretboard surface remains flat and of equal thickness all around.

{{<imgresize sanding_block.jpg "800x800" "Using the radius sanding block">}}

This is followed up with 220 grit.

The fretboard with a nice 12 inch radius applied.

{{<imgresize radius_front_view.jpg "800x800" "Radius viewed from headstock">}}

{{<imgresize radius_rear_view.jpg "800x800" "Radius viewed from body">}}

Fortunately any overly deep dot inlays have now come to the surface, and the fret-slot scratch at the 20th fret has completely disappeared.
