This small triangle of the cherry wood broke out during routing.  It was probably caused by an existing hairline crack in the end of the board.

{{<imgresize chunk.jpg "800x800" "Missing wood">}}

I found a small matching piece of the cherry wood used for the body that fits into the corner pretty well.  This is glued and secured tightly with tape overnight.

{{<imgresize gluing_2.jpg "800x800" "Gluing in the patch">}}

{{<imgresize gluing_1.jpg "800x800" "Gluing in the patch side view">}}

Then cut with a flush cut saw fairly close.

{{<imgresize flush_cut.jpg "800x800" "Patch flush cut">}}

And then carefully chiseled down flush with the body.

{{<imgresize chiseled.jpg "800x800" "Patch chiseled to shape">}}

Barely visible, except for one corner that chipped out.  I will need to fill this with sawdust/glue putty.  There are a few other very minor holes (bug or worm holes) in the cherry that have appeared which will also be filled prior to finishing.
