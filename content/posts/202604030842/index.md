The proposed location of the jack and the angle into the control cavity, by eye.

{{<imgresize hole_position_angle.jpg "800x800" "Proposed hole location and angle">}}

First a shallow 1 inch round hole to create the recess for the lip is drilled with a forstener bit.

{{<imgresize lip_drilled.jpg "800x800" "Recess lip drilled">}}

Then the remaining 7/8 hole is drilled straight through to the control cavity.

{{<imgresize hole_drilled.jpg "800x800" "Hole drilled through">}}

Test fit.  A little deep, but depth was hard to get perfect on the rounded body.

{{<imgresize mounted.jpg "800x800" "Jack socket mounted test fit">}}

The pilot holes for the screw were drilled with a hand crank drill.  I hit the body in place with the drill chuck causing a small dent, visible just below the jack mount.

{{<imgresize mounted_dent.jpg "800x800" "Dent">}}

The dent can be steamed out by wetting it and hitting it with the heat gun until the dented wood swells back out.

{{<imgresize dent_steamout.jpg "800x800" "Preparing to steam out the dent">}}

Dent all gone!

{{<imgresize dent_removed.jpg "800x800" "Dent removed">}}

Some minor chip out around the bottom will require the edges of the jack mount hole to be sanded smooth.  That is unfortunate as I would prefer a sharp edge.  But looks acceptable.
