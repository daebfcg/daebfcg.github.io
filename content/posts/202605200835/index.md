My first attempt at mounting the pickups utilized #3 screws (humbucker mounting screws) directly into the wood.  Machine screws do not hold firmly in wood, especially this small, so this did not work very well.  I had planned to inset nuts into the top of the holes for the screws to grab, but insetting nuts this small and flush was not feasible.

The updated solution uses these brass threaded inserts with #4 screws.

{{<imgresize inserts.jpg "800x800" "#4-40 brass threaded inserts and screws">}}

The inserts are screwed into 5/16 diameter holes in the floor of the pickup routes.

{{<imgresize inserts_installed.jpg "800x800" "Threaded inserts installed">}}

These provide a solid receptacle to the pickup mounting screws.   I am using small lengths of rubber tubing for the "springs" to push the pickups up against the screw pressure, providing height adjustment, and the keep them from bottoming out and crushing the wires.

{{<imgresize pickup.jpg "800x800" "Pickup at mount point">}}

Getting these inserts positioned precisely is a tedious job.  But overall I like this solution.   The hold is very solid with no risk of anything coming loose or wearing out.
